Henderson Estate Planning FAQ

Do I need estate planning if I live in Henderson?

Yes. Nevada law requires probate for estates over $25,000 in many cases. Estate planning helps your family avoid probate court, reduces taxes, and ensures your wishes are followed. Henderson's growing property values make estate planning even more important.

How much does estate planning cost in Henderson?

Estate planning costs vary based on complexity. Simple wills may cost $500-$1,500, while comprehensive estate plans with trusts typically range from $2,500-$7,500. The cost of proper planning is always less than probate fees and family disputes.

Should I use a revocable living trust in Nevada?

Many Henderson families benefit from revocable living trusts because they avoid probate, provide privacy, and continue to manage assets if you become incapacitated. Jordan Flake can explain whether a trust fits your specific situation.

What happens if I don't have an estate plan in Nevada?

Nevada's intestacy laws will determine who inherits your property, which may not match your wishes. Your family will face probate court, public records, delays, and higher costs. Minor children may need court-appointed guardians.
