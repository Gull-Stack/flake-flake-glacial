Probate FAQ

How long does probate take in Nevada?

Uncontested probate in Nevada typically takes 6-12 months. Complex estates with disputes, tax issues, or business interests may take 1-3 years. Having experienced counsel can significantly reduce delays.

Can probate be avoided in Nevada?

Yes, through proper estate planning. Revocable living trusts, joint ownership, beneficiary designations, and other strategies can help assets avoid probate. However, some assets may still require probate administration.

What are probate costs in Nevada?

Nevada probate costs include court fees, attorney fees, executor compensation, and other administrative expenses. Total costs typically range from 2-5% of the estate value, depending on complexity and whether disputes arise.

Who can contest a will in Nevada?

Only "interested persons" can contest a will in Nevada - typically spouses, children, heirs who would inherit without a will, and named beneficiaries. Contests must be filed within specific time limits after probate begins.
