Personal Injury FAQ

How long do I have to file a personal injury claim in Nevada?

Nevada has a two-year statute of limitations for most personal injury cases. However, there are exceptions and the clock may start ticking from different dates depending on your case. It's crucial to contact an attorney immediately to protect your rights.

What compensation can I recover in a personal injury case?

You may recover medical expenses (past and future), lost wages, pain and suffering, property damage, and in severe cases, punitive damages. The amount depends on the severity of your injuries, impact on your life, and degree of negligence involved.

Do I have to go to court?

Most personal injury cases settle out of court through negotiations. However, our federal trial experience means we're prepared to take your case to court if necessary to secure fair compensation. Insurance companies know this and often offer better settlements to avoid trial.

How much does it cost to hire a personal injury attorney?

We work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you pay no attorney fees unless we win your case. We advance all case costs and only recover fees from your settlement or court award. There's no upfront cost to hire our firm.
